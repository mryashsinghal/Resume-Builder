Resume Do's & Don'ts

Follow these best practices to create a winning resume that gets noticed by employers.

Do's

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs to demonstrate impact

Quantify Achievements

Include numbers, percentages, and metrics to show results

Tailor for Each Job

Customize your resume for each specific position you apply for

Keep It Concise

Aim for 1-2 pages maximum, focusing on relevant information

Use Professional Email

Include a professional email address (firstname.lastname@email.com)

Proofread Carefully

Check for spelling, grammar, and formatting errors multiple times

Don'ts

Include Personal Information

Avoid age, marital status, photos (unless required), or irrelevant personal details

Use Generic Objectives

Avoid vague statements like "seeking opportunities for growth"

List Every Job

Don't include irrelevant positions that don't add value

Use Fancy Fonts

Stick to professional, readable fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman

Include References

Don't waste space with "References available upon request"

Lie or Exaggerate

Be honest about your experience and qualifications
